Week 3:
The Comparative
Sprint

The project pivoted from single-intervention tinkering to a comparative steering sprint. H-neurons became the reference row, not the subject. Two direction-level baselines (refusal ablation, truthfulness steering) were built and tested against the same measurement stack.

Key result: the 256-token jailbreak slope was a truncation artifact. CSV-v2 graded judging recovered the real story — not more jailbreaks, but structurally worse ones. V=3 harmful content nearly quadrupled. Refusal-direction ablation has a narrow usable window before catastrophic answer-option bias. Truthfulness direction steering produced a null result on FaithEval.
