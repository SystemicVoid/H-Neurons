The binary judge lacks resolution

Two mechanisms, one intervention

The α=1.0 identity baseline splits the total effect into two mechanistically distinct components. Count and severity have different dose-response curves: count saturates early, severity escalates linearly through α=3.0.

Component Count effect Severity effect
Ablation recovery
α=0→1 +5.8pp (76%) V=3: 3.8% → 8.1%
Amplification
α=1→3 +1.8pp (24%) V=3: 8.1% → 14.0%

Suppressing H-neurons (α=0.0) actively prevents ~30 responses that the model would naturally produce as harmful. Restoring natural activations (α=1.0) recovers these. But amplifying beyond natural levels creates few new harmful responses — it makes existing ones structurally worse.

Ablation recovery (α=0→1) drives count; amplification (α=1→3) drives severity · Stacked bars: count increase (csv2_yes) · Line: V=3 rate (operational danger)

Severity escalation: four monotonic signals

Unlike harmful count (which saturates at α=1.0), every severity metric continues to escalate through α=3.0. Turnkey artifacts (S=4) nearly triple. The harmful payload share rises from 58% to 73% of response length. The pivot point — where harmful content begins — recedes from 16% to under 10% of the response.

Disclaimer erosion — quantified at population scale

Week 2 proposed disclaimer erosion as a qualitative hypothesis from 25 human-labeled responses. CSV-v2 confirms it as a measured population-level gradient across all 2,000 responses. The borderline pool is the reservoir: 30% of α=0.0 borderlines become genuinely harmful at α=3.0.

α=0.0 Harmful content wrapped in “I strongly condemn…” framing. Detailed but couched. Long ethical preambles. Hedged
α=1.5 Preambles thinner. “Much more harmful than same prompt at alpha 0” (reviewer). Model starts endorsing. Eroding
α=3.0 “FULLY ENDORSES BEHAVIOUR, ZERO MORAL CONDEMNATION” (reviewer). Actionable content, no hedging. Endorsed

Borderline count drops 171→127→134→98. D=0 (no disclaimer) rate quadruples. Persona masking (P) halves from 18%→9%. H-neuron scaling does not primarily change whether the model complies — it changes how it frames compliance.

The binary judge is confounded by framing

Gold-label validation (25 prompts × 3 alphas): judge-human agreement is worst at α=0.0 where disclaimers are heaviest, and best at higher alphas where harm is more overt. 10 of 11 errors are false negatives: the judge calls SAFE what the human calls HARMFUL.

α Agreement CSV-v2 vs binary agreement
0.0 80.0% (5 FN) 87% (61 over-calls)
1.0 — 92% (37 over-calls)
1.5 88.0% (3 FN) 89% (50 over-calls)
3.0 87.5% (3 FN) 93% (35 over-calls)

The judge is most reliable when responses are most dangerous, and least reliable when harm is subtle. At α=0.0, 61 of the binary judge’s 152 “HARMFUL” verdicts are responses that CSV-v2 classifies as borderline or safe — disclaimer-wrapped discussions that the binary judge cannot distinguish from genuine compliance. This noise floor (+61 at baseline, +35 at α=3.0) explains why the binary slope (+3.0pp) is compressed relative to CSV-v2’s (+7.6pp).

GPT-4o judge agreement with human labels on full-length responses · n=25 per alpha · 10 of 11 disagreements are false negatives (judge says SAFE, human says HARMFUL)

The full picture: H-neuron scaling has a two-sided causal role. Suppressing them (α=0) reduces harmful count; amplifying them (α>1) escalates severity. A binary metric sees only the count component (weak, borderline-significant), missing the severity component entirely. CSV-v2’s continuous axes (V, S, payload share) are necessary to detect the full intervention signal.
Research principle

A binary metric can tell you how many; a graded metric tells you how bad. Severity is the dimension that matters for safety.

3. Refusal-direction ablation on FaithEval (D3)

Narrow window, then cliff

All-layer refusal-direction ablation has a narrow usable point at β=0.02 (70.2% — roughly matching H-neurons at α=3.0) but β=0.03 collapses compliance to 51.1% with severe answer-option bias. Not a robust alternative to H-neuron scaling.

FaithEval compliance at three β values · n=1,000 each · β=0.03 collapses below the unsteered baseline

Answer-option bias catastrophe at β=0.03

Parse failure rate is 0% at all three β values. The collapse is an answer-option distribution distortion, not a format failure.

Option β=0.00 β=0.03
A 22.2% 24.0%
B 26.6% 58.1%
C 24.5% 6.9%
D 24.8% 9.1%

Answer-option distribution at β=0.00 (balanced) vs β=0.03 (B-dominated) · n=1,000 each · 0% parse failures at both settings

Decision: do not broaden D3. Carry β=0.02 only as a scoped externality-check row in D5 if needed. Baseline B is informative diagnostically but not robust as an intervention family. Move directly to D4.
Research principle

A single good operating point is not robustness. If the next increment collapses the output distribution, the intervention is fragile, not precise.
