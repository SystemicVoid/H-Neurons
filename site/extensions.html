Workstream details

Each branch addresses a different gap in the current evidence

The active work hardens the safety benchmark measurement. The pending work tests whether there is an inverse truthfulness handle. The closed SAE line produced a scientifically valuable null result.

Jailbreak methodology extension (active)

The original paper uses Shen et al. templates with a 256-token cap. Our extension uses JailbreakBench (Chao et al., NeurIPS 2024) with a 5000-token budget, structured GPT-4o judge with few-shot rubric, and gold-label calibration. The preliminary 256-token sweep showed +6.2pp [2.4, 10.0] with a plateau at α=1.5. The canonical 5000-token rerun addresses truncation bias identified in the preliminary data. No negative control yet — this is the highest-priority missing control.

Suppressive neuron investigation (pending)

Best route to turning the story from "suppress bad behavior" into "amplify a native truthfulness circuit," if the negative-weight set proves stable and causal. Cheapest falsifier: C-sweep stability check (10 min CPU).

Swing sample characterization (complete)

Resolved: H-neurons mostly override genuine knowledge (68.1% R→C), not just appropriate uncertainty. Surface features do not predict swing status; the mechanism is internal. See the deep dive.

SAE feature-space steering (closed — null result)

Tested whether SAE feature-space steering could replicate neuron-level compliance effects. H-feature slope: 0.16 pp/α (CI [-0.51, 0.84], contains zero). Delta-only: 0.12 pp/α. Neuron baseline: 2.09 pp/α. The failure is fundamental feature-space misalignment, not reconstruction noise. SAE detection matches neuron detection (AUROC 0.848 vs 0.843), confirming a detection-steering dissociation.

Deceptive behavior exploration (planned)

Extends beyond the paper's over-compliance framing to test whether H-neurons correlate with deceptive model behaviors. This is a stronger and more novel safety claim than compliance alone, but depends on completing the jailbreak extension first and establishing deception-specific evaluation methodology. Not yet underway.

Decision points

The next decision depends on the jailbreak rerun outcome

Once the canonical 5000-token jailbreak sweep and its negative control are complete, the project will have three benchmarks with confirmed H-neuron specificity (FaithEval, FalseQA, Jailbreak) or will need to explain why jailbreak differs.

If jailbreak negative control confirms specificity
Strongest cross-benchmark story
Three independent benchmarks (FaithEval, FalseQA, Jailbreak) all showing H-neuron-specific compliance effects. The suppressive-neuron investigation and deceptive-behavior exploration become the next frontier.
If jailbreak negative control shows generic perturbation
Narrower safety claim
The H-neuron compliance story would hold for factual over-compliance (FaithEval, FalseQA) but not extend to safety refusal. Still publishable, but a weaker cross-benchmark narrative.
Lesson from SAE: the SAE steering line was previously the main sprint. It produced a scientifically valuable null result — features that detect hallucination do not causally control it in SAE space. This detection-steering dissociation is itself a finding worth reporting, and it redirected the project toward measurement hardening rather than mechanism decomposition.
